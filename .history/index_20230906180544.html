Remembering Europeans.

This is a website for remembering some of the greatest Europeans such as inventors, writers, scientists.

Index

  1. Archimedes (Greece) - Known for his contributions to mathematics and inventions like the Archimedes Screw.
  2. Blaise Pascal (France) - Inventor of the Pascaline, an early mechanical calculator.
  3. Daguerre, Louis (France) - Co-inventor of the daguerreotype, an early form of photography.
  4. Edison, Thomas (USA, but of Dutch descent) - Known for the phonograph and the electric light bulb.
  5. Faraday, Michael (England) - Made significant contributions to electromagnetism.
  6. Galileo Galilei (Italy) - Invented the telescope and made astronomical discoveries.
  7. Hertz, Heinrich (Germany) - Demonstrated the existence of electromagnetic waves.
  8. Joule, James Prescott (England) - Developed the concept of the mechanical equivalent of heat.
  9. Keppler, Johannes (Germany) - Developed laws of planetary motion.
  10. Lavoisier, Antoine (France) - Father of modern chemistry, discovered the role of oxygen in combustion.
  11. Morse, Samuel F. B. (USA, but of English descent) - Invented the Morse code and telegraph system.
  12. Newton, Isaac (England) - Formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation.
  13. Otto, Nikolaus (Germany) - Developed the first practical internal combustion engine.
  14. Pasteur, Louis (France) - Pioneered the fields of microbiology and vaccination.
  15. Quetelet, Adolphe (Belgium) - Known for his work in statistics and the Body Mass Index (BMI).
  16. Roentgen, Wilhelm Conrad (Germany) - Discovered X-rays.
  17. Siemens, Werner von (Germany) - Innovator in electrical engineering and founder of Siemens.
  18. Tesla, Nikola (Croatia/Austria-Hungary, but worked extensively in Europe) - Known for contributions to alternating current (AC) electricity.
  19. Urey, Harold C. (USA, but of Swiss descent) - Discovered the isotope deuterium.
  20. Volta, Alessandro (Italy) - Invented the electric battery.
  21. Watt, James (Scotland) - Developed the steam engine.
  22. Young, Thomas (England) - Made contributions to the wave theory of light.
  23. Zuse, Konrad (Germany) - Built the world's first programmable computer, the Z3.
  24. Ångström, Anders (Sweden) - Known for work in spectroscopy and the angstrom unit.
  25. Birch, John (England) - Invented the flame safety lamp for miners.
  26. Coulomb, Charles-Augustin de (France) - Known for Coulomb's law in electrostatics.
  27. Davy, Humphry (England) - Discovered several chemical elements, including sodium and potassium.
