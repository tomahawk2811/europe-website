Remembering Europe’s Greatest
Celebrating the minds that shaped history.

Inventors Artists Scientists Philosophers Writers Generals

Inventors

  1. Archimedes (Greece) - Known for his contributions to mathematics and inventions like the Archimedes Screw.
  2. Blaise Pascal (France) - Inventor of the Pascaline, an early mechanical calculator.
  3. Louis Le Prince (France) - Inventor of the first multi-lens camera and creator of the first motion picture film.
  4. Otto Lilienthal (Germany) - Known for his work on controlled flight and gliders.
  5. Louis Daguerre (France) - Co-inventor of the daguerreotype, an early form of photography.
  6. Thomas Edison (USA, of Dutch descent) - Known for the phonograph and the electric light bulb.
  7. Michael Faraday (England) - Made significant contributions to electromagnetism.
  8. Galileo Galilei (Italy) - Invented the telescope and made astronomical discoveries.
  9. Heinrich Hertz (Germany) - Demonstrated the existence of electromagnetic waves.
  10. James Prescott Joule (England) - Developed the concept of the mechanical equivalent of heat.
  11. Johannes Kepler (Germany) - Developed laws of planetary motion.
  12. Antoine Lavoisier (France) - Father of modern chemistry, discovered the role of oxygen in combustion.
  13. Isaac Newton (England) - Formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation.
  14. Nikolaus Otto (Germany) - Developed the first practical internal combustion engine.
  15. Louis Pasteur (France) - Pioneered the fields of microbiology and vaccination.
  16. Adolphe Quetelet (Belgium) - Known for his work in statistics and the Body Mass Index (BMI).
  17. Wilhelm Conrad Roentgen (Germany) - Discovered X-rays.
  18. Werner von Siemens (Germany) - Innovator in electrical engineering and founder of Siemens.
  19. Nikola Tesla (Croatia/Austria-Hungary) - Known for contributions to alternating current (AC) electricity.
  20. Harold C. Urey (USA, of Swiss descent) - Discovered the isotope deuterium.
  21. Alessandro Volta (Italy) - Invented the electric battery.
  22. James Watt (Scotland) - Developed the steam engine.
  23. Thomas Young (England) - Made contributions to the wave theory of light.
  24. Konrad Zuse (Germany) - Built the world's first programmable computer, the Z3.
  25. Anders Ångström (Sweden) - Known for work in spectroscopy and the angstrom unit.
  26. George Stephenson (England) - Developed the first successful steam locomotive.
  27. Robert Fulton (USA, of Dutch descent) - Developed the first commercially successful steamboat.
  28. Otto Lilienthal (Germany) - Known for his work on controlled flight and gliders.

Artists

  1. Vincent van Gogh (Netherlands) - Post-Impressionist painter known for works like "Starry Night."
  2. Leonardo da Vinci (Italy) - Renowned Renaissance artist and inventor, known for "Mona Lisa."
  3. Michelangelo Buonarroti (Italy) - Sculptor and painter, known for the Sistine Chapel ceiling.
  4. Raphael Sanzio (Italy) - Painter known for "The School of Athens."
  5. Edvard Munch (Norway) - Expressionist painter known for "The Scream."

Scientists

  1. Niels Bohr (Denmark) - Contributed to understanding atomic structure and quantum theory.
  2. Marie Curie (Poland/France) - Conducted pioneering research on radioactivity.
  3. Louis Pasteur (France) - Developed vaccines for rabies and anthrax.
  4. Robert Koch (Germany) - Identified the causative agents of tuberculosis and cholera.
  5. Max Planck (Germany) - Originator of quantum theory.
  6. Werner Heisenberg (Germany) - Formulated the Uncertainty Principle in quantum mechanics.
  7. Antonie van Leeuwenhoek (Netherlands) - Made groundbreaking discoveries with the microscope.
  8. Erwin Schrödinger (Austria) - Developed the Schrödinger equation in quantum mechanics.
  9. James Clerk Maxwell (Scotland) - Formulated the theory of electromagnetism.
  10. Richard Dawkins (UK) - Evolutionary biologist and author of "The Selfish Gene."
  11. Jane Goodall (UK) - Primatologist known for her work with chimpanzees.
  12. Stephen Hawking (UK) - Theoretical physicist known for his work on black holes.
  13. Rosalind Franklin (UK) - Made crucial contributions to understanding DNA structure.
  14. Hans Sloane (UK) - Naturalist and physician who contributed to early modern medicine.
  15. Elsa Naselski (Sweden) - Pioneer in the field of biochemistry and molecular genetics.
  16. Friedrich August Hayek (Austria) - Economist known for his defense of classical liberalism.
  17. Robert Hodge (UK) - Notable contributions in the field of astrophysics and cosmology.

Philosophers

  1. Plato (Greece) - Student of Socrates and teacher of Aristotle, known for his works like "The Republic."
  2. Marcus Aurelius (Roman Empire) - Ancient emperor of Rome, also stoic philospher and meditator.
  3. Aristotle (Greece) - Made significant contributions to logic, metaphysics, and ethics.
  4. Immanuel Kant (Germany) - Known for his work in epistemology and ethics, including "Critique of Pure Reason."
  5. Friedrich Nietzsche (Germany) - Known for his critique of traditional morality and philosophy.
  6. Søren Kierkegaard (Denmark) - Regarded as the father of existentialism.
  7. Martin Heidegger (Germany) - Known for his existential and phenomenological philosophy.
  8. Ludwig Wittgenstein (Austria) - Known for his work in the philosophy of language.
  9. Augustine of Hippo (Roman Empire) - Known for his works on theology and philosophy, including "Confessions."
  10. Saint Thomas Aquinas (Italy) - Known for his synthesis of Aristotelian philosophy with Christian theology.
  11. Niccolò Machiavelli (Italy) - Known for his political treatise "The Prince."

Writers

  1. William Shakespeare (England) - Renowned playwright and poet known for works like "Hamlet" and "Romeo and Juliet."
  2. Charles Dickens (England) - Known for his novels such as "A Tale of Two Cities" and "Great Expectations."
  3. George Orwell (England) - Known for his novels "1984" and "Animal Farm."
  4. Charles Darwin (England) - Known for his scientific literature book "On the Origin of Species".

Generals

  1. Napoleon Bonaparte (France) - Military leader who rose to prominence during the French Revolution and its associated wars.
  2. Duke of Wellington (United Kingdom) - Defeated Napoleon at the Battle of Waterloo.
  3. George S. Patton (USA, of European descent) - Noted for his leadership during World War II.
  4. Otto von Bismarck (Germany) - Key figure in the unification of Germany and known for his diplomatic and military strategies.
  5. Gustavus Adolphus (Sweden) - Known for his military reforms and leadership during the Thirty Years' War.
  6. Charles Martel (France) - Defeated the Moors at the Battle of Tours, halting their advance into Europe.
  7. André Masséna (France) - Prominent marshal under Napoleon Bonaparte.
